About Me

I’m passionate about learning and exploring connections between different fields — from Philosophy, Logic, Mathematics, and Computer Science to Cognitive Science, the Cold War period, and Quant Finance.

Music is one of my strongest inspirations: I listen to everything from classical and impressionist pieces to trip-hop and black metal, always finding new ways that sound can shape thought and emotion. Quant Finance is another constant in my life, offering unique insights into risk, models, and how theory translates into markets.

Alongside these interests, I have a background in Corporate Finance, Investments and Treasury, where I worked with investment & risk management, corporate strategy, and number-crunching on a daily basis. Over time I felt drawn toward broader intellectual pursuits — building a personal library, studying logic, computability, complexity and data science, and experimenting with Python projects that bring theory into practice.

For me, learning is not just professional growth — it’s a hobby, a passion, and a way of life. I believe in curiosity, creativity, and the balance between reason and imagination.
